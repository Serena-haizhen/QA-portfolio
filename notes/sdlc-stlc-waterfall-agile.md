# SDLC, STLC, Waterfall and Agile

## SDLC
SDLC is the software development life cycle.
It covers the whole process from idea, requirements, design, development, testing, release, to maintenance.

## STLC
STLC is the software testing life cycle.
It focuses on how testing is carried out, including requirement understanding, test design, test execution, defect reporting, regression testing, and test summary.

## Waterfall
Waterfall is a linear development model.
One phase is usually completed before moving to the next, and testing is often concentrated after development.

## Agile
Agile is an iterative development model.
It emphasizes small iterations, frequent communication, continuous feedback, and earlier testing involvement.

## Tester Involvement in Waterfall
A tester can review requirements and prepare tests early, but most test execution usually happens after development is completed.

## Tester Involvement in Agile
A tester usually gets involved from the beginning, joins requirement discussions, reviews acceptance criteria, designs tests early, and tests continuously during development.
